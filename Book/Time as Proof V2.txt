CHAPTER 3: THE MATHEMATICS OF WHAT LASTS
FROM PATTERN TO FORMULA
This chapter exists for one reason: to make time measurable without destroying its meaning.

We've seen the problem. We've felt the disconnect. Now let's build the solution.
The universal formula emerged not from theory, but from observation repeated across domains:
Impact = Saturation × log₁₀(Validation + 1) × Structural Resistance
We'll call these S, V, and R—or in their measured forms: Saturation Index, Temporal Validation Score (TVS), and Structural Resistance Coefficient (SRC).
This isn't academic abstraction. This is measurement distilled to its essentials.
Three components. One outcome: What survives.
________________


COMPONENT 1: SATURATION (S)
Not: "How big is it?"
But: "How unavoidable was it in its moment?"
The Core Calculation
Saturation = (V / A) / U × C
Where:
* V = Volume (views, users, adoption, citations)
* A = Account/Entity Multiplication (duplication, inflation, churn)
* U = Addressable Universe at the time
* C = Cross-Context Coefficient (did it escape its native environment?)
Why This Matters
Charlie Bit My Finger (2007):
* 880M views seems smaller than modern viral hits
* But in 2007, YouTube had 100M users → 8.8× platform saturation
* Today's TikTok with 50M views among 1.5B users → 3.3% saturation
Absolute numbers lie. Percentages of available attention don't.
________________


COMPONENT 2: TEMPORAL VALIDATION (V/TVS)
Not: "Is it still around?"
But: "Does it keep resurfacing?"
The Calculation
TVS = min(P, 180) × R × L
Where:
* P = Persistence in months (capped at 180 = 15 years)
* R = Resurfacing Rate (weighted events per year, normalized 0-2+)
* L = Legacy Coefficient (1.0 to 3.0+)
The Resurfacing Engine
A thing isn't validated just by existing. It's validated by repeatedly mattering.
Weighted events (examples):
* News coverage: 1.0
* Platform trend revival: 1.5
* Academic/documentary reference: 2.0
* Cultural lexicon entry: 2.5
* Historical artifact status: 3.0
Example: Numa Numa (2004) resurfaces 0.65 times per year—meaningfully, for 20+ years. That's validation.
Normalization note: R is calculated as (total weighted events) / (years since creation), capped for practical analysis.
________________

THE LOGARITHMIC INSIGHT
Why log₁₀(Validation + 1)?
Because validation has diminishing returns:
* TVS = 10 → log₁₀(11) = 1.04
* TVS = 100 → log₁₀(101) = 2.00
* TVS = 1000 → log₁₀(1001) = 3.00
Interpretation: 10× more validation → 2× impact multiplier.
The first decade of validation matters most.

ZERO-LOCKING PROPERTY
Critical design feature:
If Saturation = 0 → Impact = 0
If TVS = 0 → log₁₀(1) = 0 → Impact = 0
Philosophical implication:
No amount of views compensates for zero temporal validation.
No amount of time validates zero saturation.
Both required. That's the integrity check.
