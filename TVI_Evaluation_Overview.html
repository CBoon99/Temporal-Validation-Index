What TVI Is

TVI (Temporal Validation Index) is a proprietary analytical framework for measuring long-term impact, persistence, and structural durability across cultural content, datasets, business methodologies, and investments.

Unlike short-horizon engagement metrics that measure attention, TVI measures what survives. It quantifies temporal validation—the degree to which something becomes a structural foundation rather than passing noise. TVI distinguishes signal from noise by applying time as the ultimate validator: what persists after hype, cycles, and platform churn has burned away.

The framework operates on the principle that durability is measurable, and that structural resistance—the difficulty of spreading in a given era—correlates with long-term value. TVI adjusts for era effects, saturation dynamics, and validation patterns to produce a single index that answers: "Will this still matter after visibility fades?"

Purpose of Evaluation Access

Evaluation access exists to enable organizations to assess TVI's relevance and fit for their analytical needs. It provides insight-focused exposure to TVI's approach, outputs, and interpretive frameworks without granting production or commercial usage rights.

Evaluation is designed for internal assessment: understanding how TVI might inform decision-making, where it aligns with existing analytical workflows, and whether it addresses specific measurement challenges. It is not a trial period for production use, nor does it imply any commitment to licensing.

What Evaluation Access Includes

  • Demonstrative tools: Access to public calculator and evaluation-grade interfaces that illustrate TVI's analytical approach
  • Higher-resolution insight views: Pattern-level analysis showing how TVI distinguishes durable signals from temporary noise across domains
  • Limited multi-entity comparison: Comparative analysis capabilities for evaluating relative temporal validation across entities within the same domain
  • Interpretive summaries: Pattern-level outputs and summaries that explain TVI classifications and their implications
  • Framework documentation: High-level methodology descriptions, use case examples, and validation evidence

All evaluation outputs are conceptual and illustrative. They demonstrate TVI's analytical approach without exposing computation logic or enabling replication.

What Evaluation Access Does NOT Include

  • No formula disclosure: The proprietary TVI calculation formula, component weightings, and normalization logic are not provided
  • No weights or normalization logic: Era coefficients, domain-specific adjustments, and classification thresholds remain confidential
  • No raw datasets: Underlying data used for validation or calibration is not shared
  • No exports for client or investment use: Evaluation outputs cannot be used in client deliverables, investment decisions, or external reporting
  • No API, batch, or automation: Programmatic access, bulk analysis, or integration capabilities are not included
  • No redistribution: Evaluation materials and outputs cannot be shared, published, or redistributed without explicit permission

Demonstrative vs Decision-Grade Use

It is critical to distinguish between demonstrative and decision-grade usage:

Demonstrative use (public calculator + evaluation access): Outputs are illustrative, designed for pattern recognition and long-horizon comparison. They demonstrate TVI's analytical approach but are not licensed for operational decision-making, investment analysis, client deliverables, or any use where TVI scores inform business outcomes.

Decision-grade use (licensed access): Licensed outputs are validated, calibrated, and appropriate for operational use. They include full formula access, custom domain configurations, API endpoints, and commercial usage rights. Decision-grade usage requires a commercial license.

Evaluation access provides demonstrative outputs only. Any use of TVI for decision-making, client work, or operational systems requires commercial licensing.
