METHODOLOGY: THE ISPA FRAMEWORK2.1 Theoretical FoundationInvestment Staying Power Analysis adapts temporal validation principles from cultural impact measurement [van der Linden, 2026] to equity markets. Core mathematical structure:ISPS = MSI × log₁₀(TVS + 1) × SRC
Where:
- ISPS = Investment Staying Power Score
- MSI = Market Saturation Index
- TVS = Temporal Validation Score
- SRC = Structural Resistance CoefficientDesign principles:1. Multiplicative Structure
Zero saturation → ISPS = 0 (no market presence)
Zero validation → ISPS = 0 (unproven staying power)
Both dimensions required for foundation status
2. Logarithmic Temporal Scaling
Prevents exponential age bias
30-year track record doesn't automatically score 2x higher than 15 years
Diminishing marginal returns to longevity
Recent companies can achieve moderate scores with exceptional performance
3. Era Normalization
Founding a dominant company in 1976 (Apple: no internet, limited VC, nascent market) fundamentally harder than 2019 (WeWork: abundant capital, digital distribution, mature ecosystem)
SRC adjusts for structural difficulty across technological/competitive eras
Enables fair comparison of pre-internet vs. digital-native companies

Maintained or grew market share in core category
Sustained margins within 15% of peak (not distress pricing)
Continued innovation/product launches
Analyst coverage remains positive/neutral on competitive position
Cap rationale: 180 months (15 years) represents sufficient temporal validation. Companies sustaining 30+ years receive full credit at 180-month cap, preventing excessive age bias.Examples:
Apple: 180 (premium brand sustained 40+ years, capped)
Microsoft: 180 (enterprise dominance 35+ years, capped)
Netflix: 144 (12 years streaming dominance 2012-2024)
Tesla: 120 (10 years profitability/category leadership 2014-2024)
Peloton: 36 (3 years relevance 2019-2022, then collapse)
Rivian: 12 (1 year production, minimal validation)
Variable: Crisis Survival (0.0-3.5 scale)Number and severity of existential crises navigated while maintaining or improving competitive position.Scoring rubric:3.5: Survived 3+ crises, emerged stronger each time
Near-death experiences → market leadership
Examples:
Microsoft: Antitrust breakup threat (2000) → survived, missed mobile (2010) → recovered, cloud skepticism (2014) → now cloud leader

2.5-3.0: Survived 2-3 major crises
Significant threats to business model, successfully adapted
Examples:
Apple: Near-bankruptcy (1997) → iPhone pivot, Jobs death (2011) → Cook succession, China tensions (2019+) → services diversification

Demonstrated resilience but limited track record
Examples:
Tesla: Production hell (2018) → scaled successfully, pandemic (2020) → accelerated

Too recent for major crisis (recent IPOs)

Examples:

0.0-0.3: Failed at first crisis
Collapsed during initial challenge
Examples:

Maintains >60% share or pricing power decades later
Examples:

Took existing market, transformed it fundamentally

First or early mover, established blueprint
Others successfully copied model
Still leader but challenged
Examples:

2.0: Strong but not definitive position
Top 3 in category
Clear differentiation but not category-defining
Examples:
NVIDIA: Dominant GPUs but AMD viable, Intel entering
Meta: Facebook declining, Instagram/WhatsApp strong but TikTok rival
1.0-1.5: Follower in category
Me-too product in established space
Differentiated slightly but not category leadership
Examples:
Snowflake: Cloud data warehouse, but Databricks/BigQuery/Redshift competitors

0.5: Commodity or declining position
Weak differentiation
Losing category relevance
Examples:

LIMITATIONS AND CONSIDERATIONS8.1 Temporal BiasChallenge: Recent IPOs (2020+) cannot achieve high ISPS regardless of quality due to insufficient temporal validation.Example:
Rivian (2021 IPO): ISPS 1.3 (Ephemeral) → Correct prediction (-70%)
But hypothetically, if next Tesla: Would score low for 5-10 years despite being foundation
Mitigation:
Flag recent positions (<5 years public) as "pending validation"
Provide preliminary ISPS with confidence intervals
Track leading indicators: TAM size, gross margins, capital efficiency for early assessment
Compare to historical analogues (TSLA ISPS trajectory 2010-2015 vs RIVN 2021-2026)
In practice: Recent positions should be <10% of portfolio regardless, so false negatives on rare "next Tesla" acceptable vs avoiding many Pelotons8.2 Sector-Specific DynamicsChallenge: Technology companies face faster disruption cycles than consumer staples, utilities, industrials.Observation: Our backtest focused on technology-heavy portfolio. Staples (Procter & Gamble, Coca-Cola) might score extremely high ISPS (100+ year brands) but generate lower returns due to mature markets.Mitigation:
Develop sector-specific ISPS benchmarks
Technology Foundation: ISPS >1,000

Compare within-sector rather than absolute scores
